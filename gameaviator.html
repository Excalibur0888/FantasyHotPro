Aviator Fantasy Game Rules

1. Team Formation

To participate in the Aviator Fantasy Game, you need to assemble a team of players based on their performance in the game. A well-balanced team will give you the best chances to excel:

  • 1 Pilot: The primary player who will lead your team, influencing flight duration and potential multipliers.
  • 2-4 Co-Pilots: Additional players who contribute to your team's overall success through strategic boosts and flight adjustments.
  • 2-4 Engineers: Crew members responsible for improving aircraft stability and decreasing risk, making sure your flight lasts longer.
  • 1-2 Observers: These players can assist in predicting the ideal moment to cash out or apply boosts to maximize your team’s earnings.
  • Bench Players: Reserve players that can be substituted in based on performance or tactical requirements.

2. Scoring System

Points are earned based on the performance of your players in the game. Here’s how the points system works:

  • Flight Points:
    • 5 points for each successful flight completed by a pilot (successful landing or exit).
    • 2 points for each increase in multiplier from a co-pilot's action.
    • 1 point for each 10% increase in aircraft speed or stability.
  • Strategic Points:
    • 3 points for using a boost effectively at the right time.
    • 2 points for a successful tactical decision (e.g., predicting a safe landing time).
    • 1 point for each successful prediction made by an observer.
  • Teamwork Points:
    • 2 points for each teamwork boost applied (e.g., coordinated actions between the team members).
    • Bonus points for exceeding a certain multiplier threshold (based on the team's performance).
  • Penalties:
    • -1 point for a failed flight or crash.
    • -3 points for using a boost incorrectly (wasting potential earnings).
    • -2 points for a poor prediction or mistake in timing.

3. Captain and Vice-Captain

Selecting your captain and vice-captain is crucial for maximizing your points. The captain earns 2x points, while the vice-captain earns 1.5x points. Choose carefully based on the player's flight history, team synergy, and ability to boost the game’s multipliers.

4. Substitutions and Transfers

You can make substitutions before a match starts, swapping players to match the game’s conditions and the team’s needs. Some leagues may also allow transfers between game rounds, so use this feature to strengthen your team and stay ahead of your competition.
